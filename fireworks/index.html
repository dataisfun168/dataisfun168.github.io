Illegal Fireworks in NYC

Fireworks are not just on the 4th of July

By Christine

With summer comes warm weather and spending more time outdoors. It’s also a time for firework shows. Fireworks are expected on the 4th of July, but based on 311 complaint data illegal fireworks are happening throughout the summer.

The 311 system allows individuals to report complaints on various topics including illegal fireworks complaints occurring in the 5 boroughs of NYC. Individuals can report via phone by calling 311 and speaking to an operator, or online via a web form. Regardless of the reporting method used, complaints are available in the 311 dataset published on the NYC open data portal.

New Yorkers had a lot of complaints. From the period from May 1st - July 10th there were over 8,000 complaints about illegal fireworks. Many of the complaints occurred on the 4th of July, but many complaints happened on non-holidays.

Fireworks occurred throughout the city and complaints were logged at various times per day. Individuals can report complaints 24/7, and it's not surprising that the most complaints for fireworks happen at night. As seen in the graph most complaints happen at 10 PM suggesting that individuals are reporting as they happen, not waiting till morning to report.

Most complaints are received on Thursday. Though it’s important to note that the 4th of July fell on a Thursday so there are more complaints on Thursday night.

In a vibrant city, fireworks complaints are just one of the many complaints New Yorkers logged in the 311 system. Complaints happen throughout the summer, but unsurprisingly the 4th of July had the most complaints for illegal fireworks.
